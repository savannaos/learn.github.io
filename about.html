About

LearnML hosts hands-on coding data science exercises and accompanying videos as part of my Georgia Tech OMSCS Educational Technology project.

Motivation

Not all learning preferences are being met in Computer Science education. This limitation contributes to an exclusive environment that limits diversity in the field. Women especially prefer hands-on, social, tangible, and impactful learning, which programming can definitely provide! This project uses data analysis and machine learning exercises to measure effectiveness of these learning approaches.

Principles

  • Programming is a shared feat! Move away from the isolationist culture of programming with paired programming and team discussions in order to foster social learning.
  • Apply what you learn! Students engaged in active learning can apply and test what they know.
  • Draw connections! Relate exercises to their own experiences and to real-world problems in order understand where programmming fits in.
  • Reflect! Students who use metacognitive thinking can evaluate and guide their own learning
  • Consistent feedback! Assses student understanding in order to scaffold the learning process
